## 3. WHAT IS AN INFORMATION SYSTEM?

### Definition:
**Set of interrelated components** that collect, process, store, and distribute information for decision-making and control

### Three Basic Activities:

1. **INPUT**: Captures raw data
   - Example: Disney - airline bookings, weather data, crowd images

2. **PROCESSING**: Converts to meaningful form
   - Example: Disney - calculates attendance, wait times

3. **OUTPUT**: Transfers processed information
   - Example: Disney - reports on efficiency, profitability

4. **FEEDBACK**: Output returned for evaluation

### Data vs Information:
- **Data**: Raw facts (barcode scans)
- **Information**: Meaningful data (total sales of dish detergent by brand)

## 4. THREE DIMENSIONS OF IS

### A. ORGANIZATION Dimension:

**Structure:**
- **Senior Management**: Long-range strategy, financial performance
- **Middle Management**: Programs and plans
- **Operational Management**: Daily activities
- **Knowledge Workers**: Design products (engineers, architects)
- **Data Workers**: Scheduling, communications (secretaries)
- **Production Workers**: Produce/deliver product

**Business Functions:**
- Sales & Marketing
- Manufacturing & Production
- Finance & Accounting
- Human Resources

**Business Processes:**
- Formal rules for tasks
- Example: How customer receives credit, how billing works

**Culture:**
- Fundamental assumptions and values
- Example: UPS culture = customer service first

**Politics:**
- Different interests and viewpoints
- Conflicts over resources and rewards

### B. MANAGEMENT Dimension:

**Responsibilities:**
- Perceive business challenges
- Set organizational strategy
- Allocate resources
- Exercise leadership
- Create new products/services

### C. TECHNOLOGY Dimension:

**Hardware:**
- Computers, mobile devices
- Input/output/storage devices
- Telecommunications equipment

**Software:**
- Preprogrammed instructions
- Operating systems, applications

**Data Management:**
- Organization of data on storage media

**Networking:**
- **Internet**: Global network of networks (2.3B users)
- **Intranet**: Internal corporate network
- **Extranet**: Private network for external partners
- **World Wide Web**: Service for storing/displaying information

## 7. CONTEMPORARY APPROACHES TO IS

## CASE STUDY: Mashaweer (Egypt)

### Business Model:
- Personal assistant service (motorcycle riders)
- Runs errands 24/7 (groceries, bills, courier)
- Saves time in traffic-heavy Cairo/Alexandria
- 600 orders/day

### Technology Investment:
- Founded **Innov8** (IT company) to build custom ERP
- **Mashaweer Server**: Manages orders, routes, clients, cash, assets
- **API**: Integration with third parties
- **PDA App**: Order tracking, invoicing, barcode scanning, GPS
- **Cloud computing**: LinkDotNet + Mobinil
- **CISCO call center**: 300 IP phones, recording system

### Strategy:
- Started with $15K (3 motorcycles, 6 riders)
- Expanded during 2011 Revolution (bought assets cheap)
- Raised $1.67M for Cairo expansion
- In-house everything (no outsourcing) for quality control
